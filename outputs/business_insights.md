# Business Insights — Executive Sales Dashboard

Dataset: dashboard_sales_data.xlsx | 4,200 orders | Jan 2024 – Dec 2025

---

### 1. Sales Trend
**Observation:** Revenue is growing year over year, but profit isn't keeping pace.
**Evidence:** 2024 sales ₹106.2M → 2025 sales ₹110.8M (+4.3%). Profit ₹16.47M → ₹16.83M (+2.2%).
**Interpretation:** Margins are quietly shrinking even as the top line grows — something is eating into profitability faster than sales can offset it.
**Action:** Find what's driving the gap (discounting, cost, or mix shift) before it compounds into 2026.

---

### 2. Regional Performance
**Observation:** South sells the most, but every region earns about the same margin.
**Evidence:** South ₹64.7M revenue (15.44% margin); North ₹54.6M (15.24%); East ₹48.9M (15.55%); West ₹48.9M (15.14%).
**Interpretation:** Higher demand in South isn't being converted into a profitability edge — pricing is uniform nationwide.
**Action:** Test premium pricing in South, where demand is clearly strongest.

---

### 3. Category / Sub-Category Profitability
**Observation:** Technology powers the business; Furniture barely contributes.
**Evidence:** Technology = 18.22% margin, 70.9% of total profit. Furniture = 6.89% margin. Worst sub-categories: Tables (5.67%), Bookcases (5.71%).
**Interpretation:** Furniture consumes shelf space and logistics cost for little return.
**Action:** Review whether Tables and Bookcases are worth keeping in the current form.

---

### 4. Customer Segment Behavior
**Observation:** All three segments perform within a narrow margin band, but Corporate is the weakest.
**Evidence:** Home Office 15.51% margin (₹74.5M sales); Consumer 15.34% (₹71.9M); Corporate 15.18% (₹70.6M).
**Interpretation:** Corporate accounts may be receiving more favorable discounts or terms that quietly erode margin.
**Action:** Audit Corporate pricing and discount agreements.

---

### 5. Discount Impact
**Observation:** Margin falls in a straight line as discount rises — and turns negative past 30%.
**Evidence:** 0% discount = 20.56% margin → 31–50% discount = **–4.95% margin** (a net loss).
**Interpretation:** Heavy discounting isn't buying extra volume that offsets the lost margin — it's just giving profit away.
**Action:** Cap discounts at 20% company-wide; require approval for anything higher.

---

### 6. Shipping Performance
**Observation:** Most orders ship Standard Class, which is also the slowest.
**Evidence:** Standard Class = 58% of orders, 4.71 avg delivery days. Same Day = 0.40 days, still profitable (₹2.19M profit).
**Interpretation:** Faster shipping options are profitable but underused — there may be unmet demand for speed.
**Action:** Promote Same Day / First Class for high-value Technology orders.

---

### 7. Return Patterns
**Observation:** Furniture returns at more than double the rate of Technology.
**Evidence:** Furniture return rate 7.67% (88 of 1,147 orders) vs Technology 3.03% (42 of 1,384).
**Interpretation:** Combined with its already-thin margin, Furniture's return rate likely makes parts of the category close to break-even once reverse logistics are counted.
**Action:** Investigate root causes of Furniture returns (damage, quality, mismatched expectations).
